About R1

Every child deserves what only elite athletes have ever had.

Three doctorates. An Olympian. A Stanford physician. R1 was built by the people who spent their careers at the top of sport – and decided every child should have access to what they learned there.

Dr. Matt Kritz, PhD, CSCS
Founder and CEO Body pillar — movement & physical readiness

25+ years in elite human performance. 30+ sports. 40+ Olympic and World Championship medal performances. His movement competency research is the foundation of R1’s Body pillar.

He spent a career watching elite athletes have everything – readiness monitoring, mental performance coaching, recovery science – and realised none of it existed for the 40 million children playing youth sport. R1 is the fix.

The science behind R1

The ideas are decades old. The technology is new.

R1’s readiness framework was not invented for a startup. It was built over 25 years of practice with elite athletes – refined across 30+ sports, tested at Olympic and World Championship level, and published in The Art of Ready before R1 existed as a company.

The three-pillar model – Mind, Body, Energy – draws on established disciplines: sports psychology, movement science, and recovery physiology. What R1 adds is accessibility. The same monitoring that elite athletes take for granted, made available to every child.

This is a research initiative with intellectual heritage – not a thesis invented to raise capital.

Core team

Led by the people who built the science.

Three co-founders. Three pillars. Each person on this team designed the domain they lead.

Brian Alexander, MS, CMPC

Co-Founder Mind pillar — psychological readiness

Olympian (U.S. Water Polo). Certified Mental Performance Consultant. Supported 2024 Olympic bronze medal performance. Brian designed R1’s psychological readiness framework and the challenge/support profiling system that personalizes every coaching prompt.

The mental side of readiness is the one coaches can’t see. That’s why it’s the one that matters most to measure.

Dr. Richard Swinbourne, PhD

Co-Founder Energy pillar — sleep, nutrition & recovery

Sleep scientist and dietitian. 15+ years in elite sport. Former Singapore Sport Institute. Richard built R1’s Energy pillar – the sleep, nutrition, and recovery monitoring that determines whether an athlete can sustain their sport.

Recovery isn’t rest. It’s the system that decides whether tomorrow’s training helps or hurts.

Advisory

Research and medical advisory.

Dr. Alexandra Abbott, MD
Founding Advisor · Stanford Sports Medicine

Former collegiate athlete. Board-certified in paediatrics and sports medicine. Dr. Abbott came to medicine through kinesiology and a commitment to long-term athlete development – working with NCAA programmes, the Lakers, Dodgers, and Sparks before joining Stanford. Her focus is on young and developing athletes: evidence-based care, injury prevention, and sustainable development. She advises R1 because the mission matches her own – protecting athlete development early, before damage is done.

R1’s advisory structure is in its founding stage. As the national study scales, the advisory board will expand to include additional research, medical, and policy voices. Current governance is led by the founding team with advisory input from Dr. Abbott.

Research and institutional context

Where R1 sits.

Stanford

Stanford Sports Medicine

Founding advisor Dr. Alexandra Abbott connects R1 to one of the leading sports medicine research programmes in the country. Her advisory role ensures R1’s readiness framework meets the standards expected by medical and research communities.

Aspen

Aspen Institute / Project Play

R1’s study draws on and complements the Aspen Institute’s State of Play research – the most cited data source in US youth sport. The 63X30 coalition has set the federal target of 63% participation by 2030. R1’s readiness monitoring addresses a gap no current coalition member covers.
